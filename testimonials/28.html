EUROPE & AUSTRALIA REFERENCES (click here)

Susan Twining
(Xpat New Zealander)
Taggia, Italian Riviera
Canelli Olive Oil
email: [email protected]
Date. April 2012

Jacqui and Rod are quite fantastic! I waited sometime for their arrival knowing of Rod's reputation in NZ as an Award Winning Master Builder. My expectations/hopes were greatly exceeded as both he and Jacqui are both such skilled hard workers who are also great fun and a real pleasure to be with. They work well together and this enables them both to work to each other's strengths. Both are imaginative problem solvers who look for positive outcomes.
While at Canelli they installed a kitchen - made an IKEA kitchen into an almost 'bespoke' kitchen and cleaned, prepared and laid a reclaimed mahogany parquet floor. I had no idea of what I was asking until we began! Jacqui cleaned nearly 2000 wood tiles, Rod remedied a badly 'bowing' floor and sanded and laid the tiles. The result is wonderful.
In addition they did much else: Jacqui strimmed the terraces, faster than anyone else has ever done; Rod checked over the house and made a variety of repairs/improvements. Rod earned the nickname Archimedes for restoring the shed roof which had entirely blown off. A series of pulleys, tree pruning and the help of a Nissan Micra restored it to the right way round on the building! He then secured it properly. All this was done the morning before they left when they had planned to cycle along the coast.
I strongly recommend that any host ensures they are enabled to give their best by providing proper tools and allowing them scope to work.

We cannot believe that we have been so lucky as to have had Jacqui and Rod for three weeks.
I recommend them both without reservation and am happy to provide further references/photos of their work, etc. as required. We now hope they will come to our place in London.

Susan Twining

Host Name: Donatella, Villa Fantasia, Rome ([email protected])
Italy: October, 2011

What I love of being part of "Helpx big family" goes beyond 'work that gets done' as to me it is much more often an invaluable human experience, due to the people that I have got the chance to meet and share time with. Rod and Jacqui are one of these lucky moments, being the time spent with them pleasure, fun...and yes, a lot of work done in the merriest and most energetic atmosphere. Jacqui cleaned and prepared an old iron stove, prevailed on ivy that had been strangling trees and ruining walls for years, cut laurel bushes, prepared, loaded and unloaded endless boxes of logs happily driving my car up and down the property, while Rod, the King of Chainsaw, cut tons of wood for winter, walked on my roof fixing things - actually fixing things wherever he saw and decided was needed - repaired the small door for the cats to get into the house but most of all created the best way to make that 'pot belly stove' work - and how well!- for the winter to come, deciding what to buy, how to fix everything. Expertise and creativity are his best tool: for Rod everything "is a piece of cake". And the strength and energy of Jacqui helped me in everything was needed with the loveliest smile. In all this, good laughs, nice talks, free time spent happily together sightseeing around...,so, not only 'Helpers', but new friends, who will always be more than welcomed in my home again...hope soon!
